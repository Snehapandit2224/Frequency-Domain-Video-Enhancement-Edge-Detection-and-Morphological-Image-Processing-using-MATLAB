    The Sobel operator is expected to best preserve the edges.

    Why Sobel?
    1. Noise Reduction: Sobel uses a 3x3 kernel which incorporates smoothing (weighting center pixels higher)
       concurrently with differentiation, making it very effective at suppressing the noise inherent in
       low-light photography while retaining the strong boundary changes (edges).
    2. Edge Cohesion: It generally produces strong, continuous, and well-defined edges, which is better
       for object recognition (like the sign in the image).
    
    Why others might be worse:
    - Robert: Uses a 2x2 kernel, providing minimal smoothing. It is extremely sensitive to noise and would 
      likely produce fragmented or noisy edges.
    - Laplacian: A second-order derivative highly sensitive to noise. While pre-smoothing (LoG) helps, 
      it often detects zero-crossings that correspond to small, non-significant intensity variations, 
      potentially over-segmenting the image with noise.

B. Morphological Transformation Script:
	 Which Operation Best Preserved the Content?
	The two basic operations, Erosion and Dilation, cause a direct and visible change by either shrinking
	 or expanding the object boundaries, thus losing fine details:

	1.Erosion: Removes fine details, thins lines, and generally reduces the total area of the foreground object(s).
	 This is a large, non-reversible change to the structure.

	2.Dilation: Fills gaps, thickens lines, and generally increases the total area of the foreground object(s). 
	This also changes the structure significantly.

	The two compound operations, Opening and Closing, are designed to be restorative and smoother while targeting 
	specific noise types:

	1.Opening (Erosion→Dilation): Primarily removes small, isolated bright spots (salt noise) and breaks narrow bridges.
	 While it removes noise, it can still slightly blur or simplify the contours, meaning some detail is lost.

	2.Closing (Dilation→Erosion): Primarily fills in small holes (pepper noise) and smooths object boundaries by filling gaps. 
	The process of dilation followed by erosion aims to restore the original size and shape better than the individual operations.

	Conclusion:
	
	The operation that best preserved the content of the original image is typically Closing or Opening.

	However, if the goal is maximal preservation while performing a mild cleaning/smoothing:

	Closing is often considered the most restorative because it is designed to fill small holes and close minor
	 gaps without drastically changing the 	overall size and shape of the primary object(s). It counteracts the
	 destructive effect of the initial dilation with a subsequent erosion step.

	Therefore, the final answer is generally:

	Closing (Dilation→Erosion) best preserved the content of the original image. While both Opening and Closing
	 are used for noise reduction and contour smoothing, they are less destructive than their basic counterparts
	 (Erosion and Dilation). Closing excels at filling in minor details and smoothing boundaries while maintaining 
	the overall object size, leading to the least visual distortion of the main content.
